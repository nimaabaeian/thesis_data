## Per-analysis verdicts

- **B1** — Supported (faithful implementation, not a measurement): the drive is a software integrator that self-drains at exactly 1.00x nominal (degenerate CI, as expected for a software integrator) and samples every 2.3s across 12 runs / 46 h, autonomously — incl. 2 runs with no visitors.
- **B2** — Supported (faithful implementation, not a measurement): labels are derived from level by the coded 60/25 thresholds, so transitions bracket them by implementation (acc 1.00/1.00); the non-trivial result is near-zero flapping (0 reversals) around the boundaries.
- **B3** — Supported: deficit state is associated with a state-contingent shift in robot action selection. Face-to-face hunger framing jumps 3% -> 66% (x24); feed-seeking speech acts go 1 -> 20 (deficit-only); co-present feeding pursuit 0.15 -> 0.43 with larger meals (21 -> 31); and the remote channel fires 172 proactive pings in deficit vs 0 at Full. Feeding-pursuit OR 4.9 [2.6,9.5] (person-clustered GEE; bootstrap 4.4 [2.1,8.0]). This is the deficit-to-action-selection evidence: the deficit biases goal-directed recovery behaviour in both channels. (Pings/feed-seeking are coded gates = faithful implementation; framing/pursuit/meal-size are emergent measurements of how strongly the deficit reshapes behaviour.)
- **B4** — Supported: when Starving, social-completion behaviour is strongly suppressed (turns diff -2.39, Engaged 0.08 vs 0.68; OR 0.03 [0.01,0.14], person-clustered GEE; bootstrap 0.03 [0.00,0.08]) while feeding pursuit rises (0.54 vs 0.26) — priority reallocation toward recovery, not undifferentiated disengagement. Starving n=13 (small-n; directional).
- **B5** — Supported: meal size rises with deficit {'Full': 21.0, 'Hungry': 29.0, 'Starving': 43.0}; proactive Telegram pings drew replies at 0.21 [0.15,0.26]; recovery is drive-initiated (proactive) not merely reactive.
- **B6** — Supported (exploratory): in the observed Starving episodes, 100% received a feed, 100% escaped Starving via feeding, and 100% recovered to Full via feeding (n=8). This is an operational status check, not a population recovery rate or a guarantee. Overall reliability (RQ2-c) is carried by the low long-run starvation occupancy (B7), not by these 8 episodes nor by the modest 21% remote ping-response rate: the robot seldom reached Starving because human engagement maintained the regulatory loop.
- **B7** — Supported (supporting reliability observation): modelled long-run Starving occupancy is median 1.0% [95% 0.2, 3.1%] (run-level block bootstrap; transition-level Poisson bootstrap 1.1% [0.4, 2.3%] agrees) — i.e. human feeding responses maintained the robot outside Starving for ~98-100% of the time; no absorbing state. This is not a controller-only property: the transition rates record the coupled human-robot regulatory loop, so the interpretation is that human engagement, with demonstrated participation from the drive (see B5), reliably replenished the robot. The point estimate 1.1% is fragile, so we lead with the interval; single condition means the drive's exact causal share in the feeding is not isolated. STATIONARITY CAVEAT: the CTMC is time-homogeneous, but the deployment is not — it pools visited daytime runs, idle no-visitor runs, and Phase 1/2 into one generator, so the 'long-run occupancy' is the stationary fraction of a pooled process, not of any single real operating regime. The pooling is conservative here (idle runs contribute drain/down-transitions with no feeding counterpart, pushing Starving UP), so the true visited-regime occupancy is if anything lower; read the interval as an order-of-magnitude ceiling, not a calibrated rate.
- **B9** — Supported: the affinity update process attenuates over time (mean absolute update 0.10->0.06) and is reward-coupled; it modulates salience selection through the eligibility threshold (eff_thr=max(0.50,base-0.15*affinity), exact), lowering the approach criterion for high-affinity feeders by up to ~0.14; and it gates HS2 pings to the 11/14 people above affinity 0.20. Perceptual IPS weights remain fixed. External validation: for the 12 people the robot stored under one identity, the re-threaded EMA reproduces its own persisted memory exactly (max |Δ|=0.000; an artefact independent of the event log); the 2 people the robot forked in memory are consolidated by the event log with update counts conserved, and the merge reconciles up to 0.93 of affinity fragmentation.
- **B10** — Supported: the adaptive regulatory memory tracks socially embedded interaction history and is subsequently expressed in proactive allocation. Manipulation check — obligated feeders fed 2.7x the unconstrained rate in Phase 1 [1.2,5.9] (bootstrap 2.7 [1.0,8.4]) and the no-feed pair complied perfectly (0 feeds in Phase 1), with the feeder excess shrinking 0.43x once roles lifted. Core model — +1 SD interaction duration predicts affinity change +0.17 [+0.12,+0.21] (bootstrap +0.17 [+0.06,+0.30]) for unconstrained people, moderated by role (for obligated feeders, reward delivery rather than chat duration dominated the update) and attenuated in Phase 2; all three dose definitions agree in sign; LOPO-stable. Downstream policy expression — prior affinity increases next-day proactive approaches 1.55x per +1 affinity [1.09,2.20] (bootstrap 1.65 [1.19,2.75]) beyond activity. Caveat: 2 people per controlled role (validation, not population inference). Repair-robustness — excluding the 2 canonicalization-merged identities (whose affinity was partly reconstructed) leaves the duration slope at +0.23 [+0.17,+0.29] (p=7.4e-14), so the association is not induced by the EMA re-threading.

## Reading of the four homeostatic functions

- **RQ1-1 monitoring & RQ1-2 detection** are *implementation-verification* results, not empirical measurements: the stomach level is a software integrator and the HS labels are derived from it by the same thresholds, so drain=nominal (degenerate CI, as expected for a software integrator) and 1.00/1.00 bracketing hold by implementation. The non-trivial parts are the dense autonomous sampling and near-zero flapping.
- **The drive is a two-threshold regulatory policy, not a smooth ramp** (B3+B4 read together). At the deficit line (60, entering Hungry) action selection is biased toward the recovery policy (B3): deficit vs Full shifts hunger framing 3%->67%, activates feed-seeking acts and proactive Telegram pings (0 at Full -> 172 in deficit), and increases feeding pursuit 0.15->0.43 and meal size 21->31 — a state-contingent change in behavioural allocation across face-to-face and remote channels. At the starving line (25), the controller reallocates priority away from social completion (B4): turns decrease 2.5->0.2 and Engaged completion 0.68->0.08. The empirical weight is here, in RQ2-c, the D1 ablation, and B9 — not in RQ1-1/1-2.
- **RQ2 — the HRI regulatory loop closes at the deployment level.** Across the deployment, human feeding responses to hunger signalling kept the robot's energy within the regulated range and it was out of starvation ~99% of the time (B7). That low occupancy is a *loop-level outcome* of human engagement, not a controller-only property. Caveat: single condition (the drive's exact causal share in the feeding is not isolated) and feeding concentrated among a few users (D4).

## Key quantities

- Passive drain: exactly 1.00x nominal (software integrator); dense sampling (median gap 2.3 s) across 12 monitored runs, 10 with visitors.
- Long-run Starving occupancy (RQ2-c, supporting observation): bootstrap median 1.0% [95% 0.2, 3.1%] — human feeding responses kept the robot's energy out of starvation ~97%+ of the time (loop-level outcome, not a controller-only property). Stationarity caveat: a time-homogeneous CTMC pools visited/idle runs and both phases, so read this as a conservative order-of-magnitude ceiling, not a calibrated rate (idle drain-only runs push it up).
- Observed Starving episodes: 8/8 received a feed, 8/8 escaped Starving via feeding, and 8/8 recovered to Full via feeding. This is exploratory; reliability is carried by the low occupancy above (human engagement maintaining the regulatory loop), not by these episodes or the modest 21% ping-response rate.
- Meal size by deficit: Full 21 / Hungry 29 / Starving 43 (graded expression).
- D1 grouped-CV ablation: adding hunger state changes Engaged-prediction AUC 0.669→0.757 (+0.088) and PR-AUC 0.742→0.810 (+0.068); B9: affinity learning attenuates over time and gates Hungry-state proactive pings by affinity.
- RQ3 (B10): Phase-1 manipulation validated (feeders 2.7x meal rate [1.2,5.9]; no-feed pair 0 feeds); +1 SD interaction duration -> Δaffinity +0.17 [+0.12,+0.21] (role/phase-moderated, dose-definitions agree); prior affinity increases next-day proactive approaches 1.55x [1.09,2.20] — the adaptive regulatory memory tracks interaction history and is behaviourally expressed (2 people per controlled role: validation, not population inference).

## Scope & next steps

- **Scope of generalization.** One robot, one site, 8 session-days, 12 runs, 14 named people (convenience sample). Every result is a within-deployment characterization of *this* HRI loop; none of it is a population estimate across robots, sites, or user cohorts. Read the effect sizes as existence-and-magnitude evidence for this system, not as calibrated rates that transfer.
- **Next steps.** More people per controlled role (RQ3 currently rests on 2/role) and a multi-site replication to probe generalization.
